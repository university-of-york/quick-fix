Hannah Ainsworth
Research Fellow

Profile

Biography

Hannah Ainsworth is a Research Fellow in the York Trials Unit, Department of Health Sciences. Her interests include using experimental methods (randomised controlled trials and quasi-experiments) to evaluate interventions in Public Health, Social Policy and Education. She has been involved in a number of randomised experiments evaluating interventions such as, hypnosis for reducing exam anxiety, a computer package for increasing nurse’s numeracy skills, and individual tuition for children struggling with maths.

Qualifications

  • First class BA (Hons) Politics and Social Policy
  • MSc Health Services Research

Research

Overview

Hannah's research interests include public health interventions delivered in non-NHS settings, educational interventions and health or education interventions delivered in schools.

Projects

  • MCLASS (Muslim Communities Learning About Second-hand Smoke – Pilot Trial)
  • ASSSIST (Autism Spectrum Social Stories in Schools Trial – Pilot Trial)
  • SWITCH 2 (Research led Project: Health Literacy Knowledge Exchange)
  • PIP (Pre-schoolers in the Playground – Pilot Trial)
  • Education Endowment Fund Transitions Writing Projects – 3 randomised trials
  • Independent Evaluation of the Third Space Learning’s Online Maths Tuition Service – A cluster randomised controlled trial
