Practice Education Support

The Department of Health Sciences Practice Education Support Team works with our practice partners to support our students' practice education activity.  We allocate students' practice experience, provide advice on practice learning concerns and support both students and practice partners in aspects of mentorship and the practical assessment process.

Overview

This section provides students, mentors and link lecturers with information regarding allocation and support of practice experience and answers some commonly asked questions.  Unless stated, this information is applicable to both nursing and midwifery students.

Welcome to the Department of Health Sciences Practice Education Support website.  This website provides information, advice and guidance that we hope will help make practice experience as effective as possible.

The site is mainly aimed at people who are directly involved in practice education activities.  This includes:

Students - on pre-registration nursing and midwifery programmes at the University of York.

Mentors supporting University of York students within the NHS and non-NHS settings.

Link Lecturers supporting learning in practice placements on behalf of the University of York.

While the site has not been designed for users of health services, it may still be useful for members of the public who are interested in nurse and midwifery education and would like to learn more about how practice education is supported.

Quality in Practice

Students spend 50% of their programme in practice so it is important that this experience meets the standards required by both professional and funding bodies such as the Nursing and Midwifery Council and the Strategic Health Authority (soon to be Local Education and Training Boards).

Ensuring the quality of the practice experience is a joint responsibility between the practice provider, the student and the University.  One way this is achieved is through the audit process where practice, the student and the university link lecturer come together regularly to review the learning experience, look at examples of good practice and consider any actions for future development.  The audit tool used can be found on the Yorkshire and Humber Health Education England website and the process is outlined in the Audit Process Document. (MS Word , 55kb).

The Department of Health Sciences at the University of York also has a Short Audit Document that should be completed at the point of audit and returned (via the link lecturer) to Sarah Wooffitt in the Allocations Office. This form can be downloaded from the following link:

Practice Education Support Team

The practice education support team provides advice on practice experience from both an administrative and academic perspective. At the moment we consist of 7 members of staff with a remit to co-ordinate the many aspects of practice support within the Department.

  • Fiona McInnes
  • Julie Platts
  • Sue Maude
  • Sarah Wooffitt
  • Kate Rudd
  • Fiona Powling

The team work closely with the Educational Leads for Practice based in trusts and large organisations across Yorkshire and the Humber.

The Educational Leads for Practice are responsible for supporting the mentor or practice supervisor in practice. They ensure the adequate provision of quality assured placements for the full range of students undertaking educational training within the healthcare community.
