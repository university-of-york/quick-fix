Research With Impact 

Arts and Humanities research shapes our culture and society in powerful ways. We invite you to explore just some of the many ways in which the research we are pursuing at York benefits our wider community. 

Involving the community

Helping Silent Films Find a New Voice

‌

‌‌The Silents Now project is bringing our movie heritage to modern day audiences. Find out more on the Silents Now website.

Our researchers enjoy sharing their research with the public. Public engagement projects have looked at, for example, the History of TB, York's chocolate industry, the commemoration of the First World War and silent cinema.

Find out more about how you can get involved with the life of the HRC.

Preserving and interpreting the past

Restoring York Minster

‌

York researchers share their expertise in stone work and stained glass to help restore York Minster's Great East Window.

York Arts and Humanities research preserves and interprets our physical and cultural heritage. From the earliest house in England to theatre scripts from the Holocaust, York research helps inform the future by preserving and interpreting our past.

Enriching Cultural Life

De Stijl, Mondrian and Dutch Modernism

‌

York researchers worked with the Gemeentemuseum in The Hague on an exhibition showcasing the 20th century art movement led by Mondrian.

York's Arts and Humanities researchers contribute to our cultural life by creating performances, exhibitions and artworks in collaboration with our many partners. Explore a selection of exhibitions based on research collaborations with Hardwick Hall, the National Gallery and the Tate.

Sharing expertise

Forensic Speech Science

‌        

York researchers are in demand in court rooms around the world where their expertise is aiding efforts to identify and eliminate crime suspects.

York Arts and Humanities researchers share their expertise with a broad range of collaborators, including the public sector, industry, cultural organisations, charities and the media. York researchers have provided expert advice for media productions including Wartime Farm, The Duchess and Poldark.
