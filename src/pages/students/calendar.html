Erasmus+

Erasmus calendar

WhenActivity
Autumn Week 1
  • First-year students are informed about Erasmus opportunities at orientation sessions in their departments.
  • Students interested in Erasmus should start language preparation classes at Languages For All.
Autumn Week 6
  • The Centre for Global Programmes will hold its Global Opportunities Fair in conjunction with the Careers Service where you will be able to hear from returning students regarding their exchange experiences.
  • The Erasmus+ application will open on e:Vision at Global Opportunities Fair.
  • Check with your department for talks/meetings relating to next year's exchanges.
Autumn Weeks 7-10
  • Attend any departmental sessions with Departmental Erasmus Co-ordinators.
  • Research and choose an exchange destination/duration.
  • Attend workshops and talks offered by the Centre for Global Programmes - check our web pages for further details of these events.
  • Understand funding options and how the Erasmus grant works.
  • Discuss with friends and family.
  • Discuss with your Supervisor and Departmental Erasmus Co-ordinator.
Spring Term
  • Submit an Erasmus+ application via your e-vision home page by 1pm on Wednesday 25th January 2017.
  • Follow your Department's selection procedure, your Departmental Erasmus Co-ordinator will continue to counsel interested students throughout the term.
  • Once selected:
    • Attend mandatory workshops and clinics
    • Submit external applications to host institution (usually start becoming available in February)
    • Apply for Language Course where applicable
Summer Term
  • Confirm acceptance to host institution.
  • Start preparations: accommodation, insurance, language course etc.
  • Attend Pre-departure Workshops and clinics.
  • Register your exchange and apply for Erasmus grant via e:Vision home page by Wednesday 10th May 2017.
  • Confirm final list of course choices with your department and complete your Learning/Training Agreement.
  • Discuss with your Departmental Erasmus Co-ordinator how marks achieved will be treated on your return.
  • You will receive your Erasmus contract from the Centre for Global Programmes.  This must be signed and returned before you leave for the summer break.
  • Complete year with a satisfactory academic standard.
Summer Vacation
  • The Centre for Global Programmes will be available for any queries and may be in touch with students regarding different matters so it is important to keep checking your York e-mail at this time.
  • The Centre for Global Programmes will prepare first allocation of grants to students.
  • Returning students submit their final paperwork to the Centre for Global Programmes.

Worldwide Exchange

Worldwide Exchange calendar

WhenActivity
Autumn Week 1
  • First years and eligible second years will be informed via their departments about the opportunities for study abroad and who to contact.
Autumn Week 6
  • The Centre for Global Programmes will hold its Global Opportunities Fair in conjunction with the Careers Service where you will be able to hear from returning students regarding their exchange experiences.
  • Worldwide Exchange applications will open on e:Vision at Global Opportunities Fair.
Autumn Week 7-10
  • Research and choose an exchange destination, including reading the briefing notes for your chosen exchange.
  • Attend workshops and talks offered by the Centre for Global Programmes - check our web pages for further details of these events.
  • Prepare your internal application (e:Vision home page).
  • Review application with your Supervisor - students should discuss options with supervisors relating to course requirements and suitability of exchange.
  • Understand funding options and awards available.
  • Discuss with friends and family.
Spring Term
  • The closing date for internal applications for Worldwide exchange schemes via e:Vision is 1pm on Tuesday 3rd January 2017.
  • Applicants will be short listed for interviews.
  • Interviews will be held on Wednesdays in weeks 3-6.
  • Once selected students will be expected to:
    • Attend mandatory workshops and clinics.
    • Submit external applications.
    • Apply for funding where possible.
Summer Term
  • Students receive acceptance letters, ID numbers and visa documentation from host institution.
  • Confirm acceptance to host institution.
  • Start preparations: visa, accommodation, insurance, language course, inoculations, course enrolment etc.
  • Attend Pre-departure Workshops and clinics.Confirm final list of course choices with department and discuss how marks achieved will be treated on your return.
  • Complete year with an average of 60% in assessments.
Summer Vacation
  • The Centre for Global Programmes will be available for any queries and may be in touch with students regarding different matters so it is important to keep checking your York e-mail at this time.
  • Most exchange universities hold mandatory orientation events prior to the start of the first semester.
