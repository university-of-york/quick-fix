York Students in Communities Placements

Volunteer roles across the city's voluntary and community organisations.

Hear what students have said about volunteering here.

Stay up to date! Follow us on Facebook and Twitter @UoYVolunteering.

1. Browse our wide range of opportunities avaliable this term, make sure you check the key dates and commitment suits your academic timetable.

2. Apply for your ideal opportunity through the Careers Gateway in the 'Exclusive Opportunities @York' tab.

3. Receive a confirmation email and wait for a response from your community partner.

4. Attend training and get started! Confirm you have been successful in getting involved by filling in this form.

5. Enjoy your placement! When you are done, fill in the short evaluation form and gain recognition for your volunteering e.g. through the York Award.

About

Applications are open now! Please apply through the Careers Gateway by midnight, Sunday Week 5 by following the links on the opportunity briefs.

Key points

  • Placements vary in length and time commitment. Placements can range from 3-5 hours per week for one term or up to a six month commitment.
  • The communities team will pass on your application to the community partner you want to work with, who should be in touch with you shortly after applications close.
  • Decisions regarding applications are made by the individual organisations who will be in contact with you by the end of term. If you haven't heard from them by Week 10 then please contact communities@york.ac.uk, 01904 323264.

‌‌Use your interest in York Archaeological Trust to become a valuable part of the presentation of York’s heritage in one of the following roles:

  • Viking Collection Handling Guide (*) 
  • Visitor Survey Market Research Assistants
  • DIG Interpretation Assistant (*) 
  • Richard III and Henry VII Room Guides (*)

York Museums Trust - Volunteers (PDF , 352kb)

If you have an interest in the heritage sector and want to help others enjoy the history of York, why not volunteer with York Museums Trust in one of the following roles:

  • York Art Gallery -  Anthony Shaw Gallery Volunteer
  • Hands on Here! at York Art Gallery, York Castle Museum or the Yorkshire Museum (*) 
  • York Castle Museum - Kirkgate Costumed Volunteer (*) 
  • York Museum Gardens - York Observatory
  • York Castle Museum - Raindale Mill

 (*) This opportunity may involve working with children and young people.
